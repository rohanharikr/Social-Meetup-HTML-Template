About us. Among us.

She had no intention of choosing sides until she was sure who was going to win.

She yelled down at her boys who were arguing over the one swing left in the tiny playground.

He'd had a bad day and just needed something to make him feel better. The footsteps were moving away. As he opened the car door, there was a loud noise. He hadn't seen anything like it in twenty years of teaching. He turned off the light and there it was. The house wasn't the same to her any more

She'd got him wrong, but that was her loss.

It was time. She'd fought against it for so long.

The old house, with its wildly overgrown garden.
